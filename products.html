FAQ

What is the difference between a portfolio and a CV?

A portfolio and a CV serve different purposes. A CV (Curriculum Vitae) is a document that provides a detailed summary of your education, work experience, skills, and achievements. It is typically used for job applications and focuses on your professional background. On the other hand, a portfolio is a collection of your work samples, projects, and creative achievements. It showcases your skills, talents, and creativity through visual representations such as images, videos, or interactive elements. Portfolios are commonly used in creative fields such as design, art, photography, and web development to demonstrate your capabilities and style.

When should I use a portfolio

A portfolio is beneficial when you want to showcase your work visually and provide tangible evidence of your skills and expertise. It is particularly useful in creative industries where employers or clients want to see examples of your past projects or creative output. If you are applying for roles such as graphic designer, web developer, photographer, or artist, a portfolio can help you stand out and provide a comprehensive view of your abilities.

What should I include in my portfolio?

Your portfolio should include a selection of your best work that highlights your skills and expertise. It can consist of images, videos, case studies, descriptions, or interactive elements depending on the nature of your work. It's important to curate your portfolio and showcase a diverse range of projects that demonstrate your abilities and creativity effectively.
